# Business Model

## 1. Revenue Streams

| Stream | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Marketplace commission | Percentage fee on each completed booking |
| Tutor subscriptions | Optional paid plans for premium tools and visibility |
| Student subscriptions | Access to AI mock interviews, analytics, and practice packs |
| Organization plans | Hiring programs, candidate management, and reporting |
| Add-ons | Recording storage, premium assessments, extra AI credits |
| Enterprise licensing | Dedicated workspace, SSO, custom SLAs, and compliance |

## 2. Pricing Strategy

- Commission-led core marketplace for fast adoption
- Freemium entry for students and tutors
- Usage-based AI credits for compute-intensive features
- Enterprise contract pricing for organizations and institutions

## 3. Cost Structure

- Cloud infrastructure: compute, storage, bandwidth, backups
- Third-party services: payment gateways, messaging, video services, AI APIs
- Product and engineering: development, QA, DevOps, support
- Sales and marketing: acquisition, partnerships, and enterprise sales
- Compliance and security: audits, monitoring, legal, and fraud controls

## 4. Unit Economics

| Metric | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Contribution margin | Positive at marketplace scale |
| CAC payback | Under 6 months for self-serve motion |
| LTV:CAC | Greater than 3:1 |
| Gross margin | 70%+ for software-led revenue |

## 5. Customer Segments

- B2C students preparing for interviews
- B2B tutors and trainers monetizing expertise
- B2B2C organizations hiring candidates or training staff
- Enterprises needing private interview and coaching environments

## 6. Monetization Principles

- Keep the student entry point affordable
- Reward tutors with transparent commission math
- Price enterprise features around security, control, and scale
- Preserve expansion revenue through AI, analytics, and storage add-ons
