# Market Research

## 1. Market Context

Demand is driven by competitive hiring, remote work normalization, and higher expectations for interview readiness. Candidates now expect practice, feedback, and structured coaching in a digital workflow, while organizations need a controlled way to source, invite, and evaluate candidates.

## 2. Target Segments

- Fresh graduates preparing for first job interviews
- Mid-career professionals switching roles or industries
- Language and aptitude learners
- Tutors, trainers, and interview experts who sell coaching sessions
- Organizations that run hiring campaigns, interview drives, or internal assessments
- Universities, bootcamps, staffing firms, and training centers
- HR teams that need structured interview preparation and candidate tracking

## 3. Competitive Landscape

| Category | Typical Offer | Gap Addressed Here |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scheduling tools | Booking only | No coaching, billing, or AI evaluation |
| Video platforms | Live meetings | No marketplace, invitation, or revenue workflow |
| Tutoring marketplaces | Session discovery | Weak interview-specific workflows and assessment |
| AI interview apps | Automated practice | No live booking or tutor ecosystem |
| ATS / hiring tools | Candidate tracking | No coaching, session booking, or AI practice layer |

## 4. Differentiation

- Unified marketplace plus mock interview system
- Live human coaching and AI scoring in one product
- Organization invitation and hiring campaign workflow alongside the student marketplace
- Subscription, commission, and wallet monetization together
- Shared platform support for consumers, tutors, and organization accounts

## 5. Market Positioning

The platform should be positioned as an interview readiness and candidate engagement system rather than a simple tutoring app. That framing supports premium pricing, investor narrative, and expansion into organization hiring workflows.

## 6. Go-To-Market Hypothesis

- Start with students and tutors in a focused geography or niche category
- Use referral loops, tutor-led acquisition, and content-led interview preparation marketing
- Expand into organizations, bootcamps, and HR teams after activation metrics stabilize
- Add enterprise controls, candidate visibility rules, and compliance features for premium organization accounts

## 7. Revenue Opportunities

- Booking commission from each completed interview session
- Premium subscriptions for AI practice, analytics, and advanced tutor tools
- Organization plans for hiring campaigns, reports, and candidate management
- Add-on revenue for transcription, recording, certificates, and storage
- Promotional revenue through coupons, referrals, and campaign-driven upsells

## 8. Key Market Risks

- Long sales cycles for organization accounts compared with self-serve student adoption
- Trust and safety concerns around candidate data, payments, and session quality
- Feature overlap with scheduling, video, tutoring, and AI point solutions
- Risk of overbuilding enterprise workflows before consumer traction is proven
